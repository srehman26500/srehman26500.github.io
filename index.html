About

Why I write copy — and why it matters to you

I specialise in email copy and brand voice for DTC food, wellness, and lifestyle brands — and I bring the same warmth, clarity, and conversion focus to every project I take on, regardless of the niche.

Copywriting found me when I realised that every great brand I loved had one thing in common: their words felt like they came from a person, not a company. That moment changed how I see writing — not as filling space on a page, but as the single most powerful tool a brand has to make someone feel understood.

My approach is simple: understand the brand's voice deeply, understand the reader's real hesitation, and write something that bridges the two — with honesty, personality, and a clear reason to act.

✉ Re-engagement Email

01 · Re-engagement Email · Khaadi · Fashion

Spec Work
Brand: Khaadi — Pakistan's leading fashion and lifestyle brand
Objective: Re-engage customers who haven't shopped in 60+ days, promote the new Eid collection
Audience: Women aged 22–40, Pakistan & diaspora, value quality and cultural identity
This is a speculative (spec) piece created for portfolio purposes only. I have no affiliation with Khaadi. All brand assets referenced belong to their respective owners.
Subject Line: You've been quiet. So have we — until now.
Preview Text: Something new. Something made for you.

Hey [First Name],

We noticed you haven't visited in a while. We're not upset. Just a little quiet without you.

Because somewhere between the busy weeks, the chai that went cold, and the to-do lists that never end — getting dressed for yourself got pushed aside.

That changes now.

Our new Eid collection just landed — and it's everything you'd want it to be. Soft fabrics, prints that feel like home, and cuts that make the mirror worth looking at.

Come back. Your wardrobe misses you.

Here's 15% off your next order — just for you:

SHOP NOW — Use code: WELCOME15

✦ Strategic Annotation

  1. Objective: Re-engage lapsed customers (60+ days, no purchase) while introducing the Eid collection. The 15% code adds urgency without desperation.
  2. Audience Fit: Pakistani women who value emotional connection to brand and culture. The "chai that went cold" reference creates instant local recognition — it's not just copy, it's their life described back to them.
  3. Technique: Self-aware warmth opens the email ("We're not upset. Just a little quiet") — this mirrors Khaadi's human brand voice and disarms the typical "why are they emailing me?" reaction before the offer arrives.
  4. Key Creative Decision: "Your wardrobe misses you" puts the emotion on the brand's side, not the customer's — it removes guilt and replaces it with belonging. That emotional flip is what makes someone click.
  5. Expected Outcome: 18–25% click rate among re-engaged segments. Personal local tone + seasonal hook (Eid) + discount code covers re-engagement and seasonal cross-sell in one send.

02 · Social Ad · Graza · Food & Beverage

Spec Work
Brand: Graza — premium squeeze-bottle olive oil brand (US)
Objective: Cold traffic conversion — first purchase of Sizzle or Drizzle SKU
Audience: Home cooks aged 25–40, food-curious, follow food content online
This is a speculative (spec) piece created for portfolio purposes only. I have no affiliation with Graza. All brand assets referenced belong to their respective owners.

You don't think about olive oil until you have a bad one.

Then you think about it constantly.

Graza is single-origin Spanish olive oil in a squeeze bottle — because great olive oil should be easy to use, not locked in a pretty bottle you're afraid to finish.

Drizzle it on everything. Or Sizzle with it. Or both.
(We're not judging. We're encouraging.)

Your pasta deserves better. So does your breakfast egg. Honestly, so do you.

Shop Graza — Free shipping on first order

Headline: The olive oil that makes everything better.
CTA Button: Shop Now

✦ Strategic Annotation

  1. Objective: Convert cold food-interested audiences to a first purchase — top of funnel, awareness to action in one scroll.
  2. Audience Fit: Home cooks who consider food a hobby. "You don't think about olive oil until you have a bad one" is a thought they've had — naming it builds instant trust before the product is introduced.
  3. Technique: Problem-aware storytelling: name the unspoken truth → validate it → reveal the solution. The humour in "we're not judging, we're encouraging" matches Graza's playful DTC voice exactly.
  4. Key Creative Decision: "Your pasta deserves better. So does your breakfast egg. Honestly, so do you." — escalating the logic from food to self-worth makes the purchase feel like a small act of self-respect, not just grocery shopping.
  5. Expected Outcome: 2.8–4.5% CTR for food-interest cold audiences on Meta. The squeeze-bottle differentiator is named early — it answers the "why not just buy any olive oil?" objection before it forms.
🖥 Homepage Hero Copy

03 · Homepage Hero · Calm App · Wellness

Spec Work
Brand: Calm — meditation, sleep, and mental wellness app
Objective: Drive free app downloads from cold web traffic
Audience: Professionals aged 25–45, high-stress, sleep-deprived, sceptical of "wellness" language
This is a speculative (spec) piece created for portfolio purposes only. I have no affiliation with Calm. All brand assets referenced belong to their respective owners.

Meet the version of you that actually sleeps.

Calm is the app for sleep, meditation, and mental rest. Used by 100+ million people who decided that running on empty was no longer good enough.

You already know the problem.

The mind that won't switch off. The 2am thoughts that feel urgent but aren't. The morning that starts before the rest you needed.

Calm doesn't promise a perfect mind. It gives you the tools to work with the one you have — guided meditations, sleep stories, breathwork, and daily practices designed for real life, not a wellness retreat.

Less noise. More you.

Try Calm Free — No credit card required

★★★★★ Rated 4.8 stars · 1.5M+ reviews · Used in 190 countries

✦ Strategic Annotation

  1. Objective: Drive free app download from cold web traffic. Must convert a sceptical visitor in under 8 seconds.
  2. Audience Fit: High-achieving adults who dismiss "wellness" as soft. "Running on empty" and "the version of you that actually sleeps" use achievement language — they speak to identity, not illness.
  3. Technique: Future Self framing — the headline sells the outcome (sleeping version of you), not the product (an app). Lifestyle apps convert better on aspiration than features.
  4. Key Creative Decision: "The 2am thoughts that feel urgent but aren't" — one line that anyone who has lain awake anxious will immediately recognise. Hyper-specificity creates trust; the reader feels seen.
  5. Expected Outcome: 3–5% conversion rate on cold web traffic. The no-credit-card CTA removes the #1 barrier. Social proof line (stars, reviews, countries) delivers immediate trust below the action button.

04 · Product Description · Aesop · Beauty

Spec Work
Brand: Aesop — premium Australian botanical skincare
Product: Reverence Aromatique Hand Wash
Objective: Drive add-to-cart on product detail page
Audience: Affluent adults 28–50, aesthetic-conscious, sceptical of marketing hype
This is a speculative (spec) piece created for portfolio purposes only. I have no affiliation with Aesop. All brand assets referenced belong to their respective owners.

Reverence Aromatique Hand Wash

A hand-washing ritual that turns the most repeated act of your day into a small, deliberate pleasure.

Some things are worth slowing down for.

Reverence is built for the in-between moments — the pause between tasks, the transition between spaces, the thirty seconds between what came before and what comes next.

Formulated with Vetiver Root, Ylang Ylang, and Petitgrain, it lathers into a scent that sits between earth and bloom — grounding without being heavy, bright without being sharp.

The viscosity is deliberate. Not watery, not thick — enough to feel like care, not product.

It does not demand attention. But once you've used it, the absence of it is noticeable.

Key Ingredients:
Vetiver Root — grounding, woody, ancient.
Ylang Ylang — floral without sweetness.
Petitgrain — clean, citrus-adjacent, clarifying.

✦ Strategic Annotation

  1. Objective: Drive add-to-cart on Aesop's product page — primarily for returning visitors and warm email traffic already familiar with the brand.
  2. Audience Fit: Aesop buyers are intellectual and deeply sceptical of superlatives. The copy uses sensory precision over marketing claims — "sits between earth and bloom" is more persuasive to this audience than "smells amazing."
  3. Technique: Ritual Reframing — hand-washing is mundane, but framing it as a "pause" and "deliberate pleasure" elevates the purchase from transactional to aspirational. Premium brands sell the experience around the product.
  4. Key Creative Decision: "It does not demand attention. But once you've used it, the absence of it is noticeable." — the restraint makes this line powerful. It arrives after trust is established and lands like a quiet truth, not a sales pitch.
  5. Expected Outcome: Higher conversion on warm traffic and return visitors. Ritual framing also supports multi-size purchase consideration, increasing average order value.
🔍 Google Search Ad (RSA)

05 · Google Ad · HelloFresh · Food Delivery

Spec Work
Brand: HelloFresh — meal kit delivery service
Objective: Capture high-intent search traffic / first-box trial
Audience: Busy professionals and families, 28–45, comparing meal kit options
This is a speculative (spec) piece created for portfolio purposes only. I have no affiliation with HelloFresh. All brand assets referenced belong to their respective owners.

HEADLINES (Google RSA — 8 options, auto-tested):

  1. Fresh Meals. Zero Planning.
  2. Chef-Designed Recipes Delivered
  3. Skip the Grocery Store Tonight
  4. 60% Off Your First Delivery
  5. Meals Ready in Under 30 Minutes
  6. Cancel Anytime — No Commitment
  7. 25 New Recipes Every Week
  8. Less Planning. More Eating Together.

DESCRIPTIONS (3 options):

  1. Get fresh, pre-portioned ingredients and easy step-by-step recipes delivered to your door. 60% off your first box. No commitment required.
  2. Stop spending Sunday planning the week's meals. HelloFresh handles the menu so you can focus on cooking — not shopping. Try your first box for less.
  3. 25 new recipes each week. Pre-measured portions. Meals ready in 30 minutes. Join over 8 million customers who've made the switch.

✦ Strategic Annotation

  1. Objective: Capture high-intent search traffic for "meal kit delivery" queries and convert with a clear first-box discount offer.
  2. Audience Fit: Searchers are comparison-shopping. Headlines target the three main decision criteria: price (60% off), convenience (30 minutes), and no risk (cancel anytime). These three objections kill meal kit conversions — removing all three in one ad set wins.
  3. Technique: RSA format requires headlines and descriptions that work standalone AND in combination. Strategy: lead with outcomes, add proof, de-risk. Each headline serves a different psychological moment in the search journey.
  4. Key Creative Decision: "Skip the Grocery Store Tonight" targets the exact emotional moment of intent — someone searching meal kits is often dreading a grocery trip. This headline meets them precisely there.
  5. Expected Outcome: 3–6% CTR for branded + category keywords. Discount-led headline combinations typically outperform benefit-led by 15–20% for first-touch paid search.
📹 VSL Script

06 · VSL Script · Thesis Nootropics · Health

Spec Work
Brand: Thesis — personalised nootropic supplement brand
Objective: Drive first purchase via video ad — YouTube pre-roll and Meta video
Audience: Knowledge workers and founders aged 25–45, coffee-dependent, productivity-focused
This is a speculative (spec) piece created for portfolio purposes only. I have no affiliation with Thesis Nootropics. All brand assets referenced belong to their respective owners.

90-Second Script — Narrator: Warm, calm, direct

[OPEN — ambient sound, no music]

You already know what 9am feels like when everything's clicking.

Emails are clear. Decisions come fast. The work that usually takes three hours takes one.

You also know what it feels like when they're not.

When you re-read the same paragraph four times. When a simple reply sits in your drafts for an hour. When the coffee kicks in and you just... feel edgy instead of sharp.

[MUSIC ENTERS — subtle, focused]

Thesis was built for the gap between those two versions of you.

It's a personalised nootropic — which means it's not a one-size-fits-all supplement. You take a short quiz. We look at your goals, your work style, your chemistry. And we build a formula for how YOUR brain works best.

Because the cognitive edge you're looking for isn't about taking more. It's about taking the right things.

[Testimonial graphic — name + occupation]

Thousands of founders, writers, doctors, and operators have made Thesis part of how they work.

Start your personalised starter kit today — and find your formula.

Take the quiz → thesis.com/quiz

✦ Strategic Annotation

  1. Objective: Drive first-time supplement purchase through a 90-second video targeting productivity-focused professionals on YouTube and Meta.
  2. Audience Fit: The script opens by naming the mental state the audience already wants — "when everything's clicking" — then validates their worst days. No product mention until emotional identification is locked.
  3. Technique: Before/After/Bridge structure — the "good 9am" is the After they want, the "edgy coffee" is the Before they have, and Thesis is the Bridge. VSL scripts must establish empathy in the first 15 seconds or viewers skip.
  4. Key Creative Decision: "Two versions of you" frames this as a personal identity product, not a productivity pill. People pay premiums when they believe they're buying the better version of themselves.
  5. Expected Outcome: 55–70% view-through rate (25 seconds+) for retargeted audiences. The quiz-based CTA has low commitment friction while building a segmented email list for nurture sequences.
💼 LinkedIn Outreach Sequence

07 · LinkedIn DM Sequence · B2B SaaS

Spec Work
Context: Copywriter outreach to a B2B SaaS marketing director
Objective: Secure a discovery call for conversion copywriting services
Audience: Marketing directors / heads of content at Series A–B SaaS companies
This is a speculative (spec) piece created for portfolio purposes only. This sequence represents a copywriter's own outreach, not client copy.

Message 1 — Connection Request (300 characters max)

Hi [Name], noticed you're building the content function at [Company] — I write conversion copy for SaaS brands at the growth stage. Would love to connect and follow your work.

Message 2 — First Message After Connection (2–3 days later)

Hi [Name],

Thanks for connecting.

I was looking at [Company]'s onboarding emails this week — specifically the sequence from sign-up to first key action. There's a clear opportunity to tighten the copy at the moment users are deciding whether to upgrade.

I specialise in exactly this: conversion copy for the trial-to-paid journey for SaaS products. I've written sequences designed to improve trial conversion by 18–30% for companies at similar growth stages.

Not pitching anything — just thought it was worth mentioning in case it's on your radar. Happy to share some thoughts if it's useful.

[Your Name]

Message 3 — Follow-Up (5–7 days later, if no reply)

Hi [Name],

Following up briefly on my last message.

I put together a 3-point audit of [Company]'s trial onboarding sequence — nothing long, just the specific moments where I'd change the copy and why. Happy to send it over even if the timing isn't right for a project now.

Either way, looking forward to following [Company]'s growth.

[Your Name]

✦ Strategic Annotation

  1. Objective: Warm a cold B2B prospect to a discovery call through a 3-message sequence focused on adding value before pitching.
  2. Audience Fit: Marketing directors at growth-stage SaaS are saturated with generic pitches. This sequence stands out by doing homework first — referencing the specific onboarding flow — and leading with insight, not services.
  3. Technique: Observation + Offer method. Message 1 connects. Message 2 demonstrates domain knowledge with a specific, relevant observation. Message 3 offers a free audit — a low-commitment, high-value proof of work ethic.
  4. Key Creative Decision: "Not pitching anything" in Message 2 is deliberately disarming. In a world of constant pitching, saying this out loud — and meaning it — creates genuine curiosity and sets this sequence apart.
  5. Expected Outcome: 12–18% reply rate for sequences with genuine company-specific personalisation. Generic versions perform at 3–5%. The entire difference is in the research.
