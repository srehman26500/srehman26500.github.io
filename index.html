✉ Re-engagement Email

Re-engagement Email — Fashion & Lifestyle

Brief: Re-engage customers who haven't purchased in 60+ days. Promote the new Eid collection.
Audience: Pakistani women, 22–40. Value quality, cultural identity, and brands that feel personal.
Subject: You've been quiet. So have we — until now.
Preview: Something new. Something made for you.

Hey Ayesha,

We noticed you haven't visited in a while. We're not upset. Just a little quiet without you.

Because somewhere between the busy weeks, the chai that went cold, and the to-do lists that never end — getting dressed for yourself got pushed aside.

That changes now.

Our new Eid collection just landed — soft fabrics, prints that feel like home, and cuts that make the mirror worth looking at.

Come back. Your wardrobe misses you.

Here's 15% off your next order — just for you:

✦ Strategic Notes

  1. Objective: Re-engage lapsed customers while introducing the Eid collection. The discount adds urgency without desperation.
  2. Audience Fit: "The chai that went cold" creates instant local recognition — it's not copy, it's their life described back to them.
  3. Technique: Self-aware warmth opens the email — it disarms the "why are they emailing me?" reaction before the offer arrives.
  4. Key Decision: "Your wardrobe misses you" puts the emotion on the brand's side. It removes guilt and replaces it with belonging — that flip is what makes someone click.
📱 Instagram Ad

Instagram Ad — Premium Food Brand

Brief: Cold traffic conversion — first purchase from a food-curious audience.
Audience: Home cooks, 25–40. Food is a hobby. Follow food content online.

You don't think about olive oil until you have a bad one.

Then you think about it constantly.

Single-origin Spanish olive oil in a squeeze bottle — because great olive oil should be easy to use, not locked in a pretty bottle you're afraid to finish.

Drizzle it on everything. Sizzle with it. Or both.
(We're not judging. We're encouraging.)

Your pasta deserves better. So does your breakfast egg. Honestly, so do you.

✦ Strategic Notes

  1. Objective: Convert cold audiences at first scroll — awareness to purchase in one piece of copy.
  2. Audience Fit: "You don't think about olive oil until you have a bad one" is a thought they've already had. Naming it builds trust before the product appears.
  3. Technique: Problem-aware storytelling — name the unspoken truth, validate it, then reveal the solution. The humour matches a playful DTC brand voice exactly.
  4. Key Decision: Escalating from food to self-worth makes the purchase feel like a small act of self-respect — not just grocery shopping.
🖥 Homepage Hero

Homepage Hero — Wellness App

Brief: Drive free app downloads from cold web traffic.
Audience: Professionals, 25–45. High-stress, sleep-deprived, sceptical of "wellness" language.

Meet the version of you that actually sleeps.

You already know the problem.

The mind that won't switch off. The 2am thoughts that feel urgent but aren't. The morning that starts before the rest you needed.

This app doesn't promise a perfect mind. It gives you the tools to work with the one you have — guided meditations, sleep stories, breathwork, and daily practices built for real life.

Less noise. More you.

✦ Strategic Notes

  1. Objective: Drive a free download from cold web traffic. Must convert a sceptical visitor in under 8 seconds.
  2. Audience Fit: High-achievers who dismiss wellness as soft. "The version of you that actually sleeps" uses achievement language — it speaks to identity, not illness.
  3. Technique: Future Self framing — the headline sells the outcome, not the product. Lifestyle apps convert better on aspiration than features.
  4. Key Decision: "The 2am thoughts that feel urgent but aren't" — anyone who has lain awake anxious recognises this immediately. Hyper-specificity builds trust faster than any claim.
📄 Product Description

Product Description — Premium Skincare

Brief: Drive add-to-cart on a product detail page for a botanical hand wash.
Audience: Affluent adults, 28–50. Aesthetic-conscious. Sceptical of marketing hype.

Reverence Aromatique Hand Wash

A ritual that turns the most repeated act of your day into a small, deliberate pleasure.

Some things are worth slowing down for.

Built for the in-between moments — the pause between tasks, the thirty seconds between what came before and what comes next.

Formulated with Vetiver Root, Ylang Ylang, and Petitgrain, it lathers into a scent that sits between earth and bloom — grounding without being heavy, bright without being sharp.

The viscosity is deliberate. Not watery, not thick — enough to feel like care, not product.

It does not demand attention. But once you've used it, the absence of it is noticeable.

Key Ingredients:
Vetiver Root — grounding, woody, ancient.
Ylang Ylang — floral without sweetness.
Petitgrain — clean, citrus-adjacent, clarifying.

✦ Strategic Notes

  1. Objective: Drive add-to-cart on a product page — primarily for warm and returning visitors.
  2. Audience Fit: Buyers at this price point are sceptical of superlatives. Sensory precision — "sits between earth and bloom" — is more persuasive than "smells amazing."
  3. Technique: Ritual Reframing — framing hand-washing as a pause elevates a mundane act into something worth paying for. Premium brands sell the experience around the product.
  4. Key Decision: "It does not demand attention. But once you've used it, the absence of it is noticeable." Restraint makes this land. It arrives like a quiet truth, not a pitch.
📱 Facebook Ad

Facebook Ad — Organic Skincare Brand

Brief: Cold traffic conversion — drive first purchase from a Pakistani audience discovering the brand for the first time.
Audience: Pakistani women, 20–38. Conscious about ingredients. Tired of chemical-heavy products. Scroll Facebook and Instagram daily.

Your skin doesn't need more products.

It needs the right ones — made from what the earth actually intended.

Most skincare sold in Pakistan is full of things you can't pronounce, designed to fix problems they quietly cause. We took a different route.

Every ingredient is natural, every formula is made locally, and every product is designed for skin that actually lives in this climate — not imported and relabelled.

No harsh chemicals. No false promises. Just skin that finally feels like yours again.

Try your first order and feel the difference within two weeks — or we'll refund it, no questions asked.

✦ Strategic Notes

  1. Objective: Drive first purchase from cold Facebook traffic by building trust and removing risk in one scroll.
  2. Audience Fit: Pakistani women increasingly distrust chemical-heavy imported products. Calling this out directly positions the brand as the honest alternative — it builds credibility before making any claim.
  3. Technique: Problem agitation before solution. The copy names the frustration the audience already feels — then presents the brand as the logical answer, not just another option.
  4. Key Decision: "Designed for skin that actually lives in this climate" is the line that does the most work. It speaks directly to a real, unspoken frustration — that most products aren't made for us. Local relevance converts.
📹 Video Script

VSL Script — Health & Supplements

Brief: Drive first purchase via a 90-second video ad on YouTube and Meta.
Audience: Knowledge workers and founders, 25–45. Coffee-dependent. Productivity-focused.

You already know what 9am feels like when everything's clicking.

Emails are clear. Decisions come fast. The work that usually takes three hours takes one.

You also know what it feels like when they're not.

When you re-read the same paragraph four times. When a simple reply sits in your drafts for an hour. When the coffee kicks in and you just... feel edgy instead of sharp.

This was built for the gap between those two versions of you.

A personalised nootropic — not a one-size-fits-all supplement. You take a short quiz. We look at your goals, your work style, your chemistry. And we build a formula for how your brain works best.

Because the edge you're looking for isn't about taking more. It's about taking the right things.

Thousands of founders, writers, doctors, and operators have made this part of how they work.

Start your personalised starter kit today — and find your formula.

✦ Strategic Notes

  1. Objective: Drive a first-time purchase through a 90-second video. Must earn attention in the first 5 seconds or the viewer skips.
  2. Audience Fit: Opens by naming the mental state the audience already wants — then validates their worst days. No product until emotional identification is locked.
  3. Technique: Before / After / Bridge — the good 9am is the After, the edgy coffee is the Before, the product is the Bridge. VSLs must establish empathy before the pitch.
  4. Key Decision: "Two versions of you" frames this as an identity product, not a productivity pill. People pay premiums when they believe they're buying the better version of themselves.
💼 LinkedIn Outreach

Outreach Sequence — B2B SaaS

Brief: A 3-message LinkedIn sequence targeting a SaaS marketing director.
Objective: Secure a discovery call by leading with insight, not a pitch.
Audience: Marketing directors at Series A–B SaaS companies.

Message 1 — Connection Request

Hi Omar, noticed you're building the content function at NovaSoft — I write conversion copy for SaaS brands at the growth stage. Would love to connect and follow your work.

Message 2 — After Connecting (2–3 days later)

Hi Omar,

Thanks for connecting.

I was looking at NovaSoft's onboarding emails this week — specifically the sequence from sign-up to first key action. There's a clear opportunity to tighten the copy at the moment users are deciding whether to upgrade.

I specialise in exactly this: conversion copy for the trial-to-paid journey. I've written sequences designed to lift trial conversion by 18–30% for companies at similar growth stages.

Not pitching anything — just thought it was worth mentioning in case it's on your radar.

Message 3 — Follow-Up (5–7 days later, no reply)

Hi Omar,

Following up briefly.

I put together a 3-point audit of NovaSoft's trial onboarding sequence — nothing long, just the specific moments I'd change the copy and why. Happy to send it over even if the timing isn't right for a project now.

Either way, looking forward to following NovaSoft's growth.

✦ Strategic Notes

  1. Objective: Warm a cold B2B prospect to a discovery call by adding value before pitching anything.
  2. Audience Fit: SaaS marketing directors are saturated with generic pitches. This stands out by referencing a specific part of their product — insight first, services second.
  3. Technique: Observation + Offer. Message 1 connects. Message 2 demonstrates domain knowledge. Message 3 offers a free audit — low commitment, high proof of work ethic.
  4. Key Decision: "Not pitching anything" is deliberately disarming. In a world of constant cold outreach, saying this — and meaning it — creates curiosity that a standard pitch never could.
